### Current State Process Documentation
**Process Name:** Athlete Financial and Health Oversight
- **Process Description:** Currently decentralized across player agents, team doctors, and limited league oversight. Cases of financial distress or injury mismanagement are addressed reactively.
- **Process Performance:** Response rates vary; no unified tracking system. Average case response time: 21 days.
- **Problem Manifestation:** Athletes experiencing financial decline or recurring injuries often go unnoticed until severe consequences emerge.
- **Improvement Potential:** Centralized dashboards for financial/medical red flags would enhance early intervention.

### Historical Process Performance
- **Baseline Performance:** Prior to 2021, internal incident logs showed fewer escalated cases due to manual flagging by agents.
- **Performance Decline Pattern:** Since 2022, post-pandemic decentralization and retirement surges caused a 35% increase in unmanaged athlete hardship cases.
- **Contributing Factors:** Agent turnover, lack of continuity post-career, absence of system integration between medical and financial departments.
- **Previous Improvement Attempts:** A pilot financial counseling program in 2021 had a 40% engagement rate but wasn’t scaled due to budget reallocation.

## Synthesis of Organizational Evidence

### Strongest Evidence for Problem Existence
Rising financial hardship claims, stagnant athlete satisfaction scores, and continued incident escalation in legal/medical support show structural underinvestment and process fragmentation.

### Strongest Evidence for Solution Feasibility  
Historical pilot success (e.g., 2021 financial literacy initiative), available discretionary budget, and moderate systems uptime indicate realistic potential for systemic fixes.

### Evidence-Based Organizational Readiness Assessment
Although baseline engagement is low, clear departmental benchmarks, past partial successes, and available funding suggest the organization is capable of meaningful change if effort is structured and led cross-functionally.

### Critical Success Factors Based on Internal Evidence
- Centralized athlete data infrastructure
- Cross-functional coordination between finance, legal, and medical staff
- Mandatory onboarding and ongoing training for athlete-facing staff
- Leadership buy-in to budget and prioritize long-term athlete outcomes
